### Purpose
This manual translates an ancient symbol set into a practical, community-scale practice. It is not a replacement for scientific remediation, planting, or water testing. It is a coherence layer you add before the work.

The premise is simple: humans can function as living transducers. When a small group achieves internal coherence in a specific place, the land and water tend to remember their own healthy pattern.

Use this for school gardens, degraded stream banks, vacant lots, or any site you already tend.

### What You Need
- 3 to 9 people who care about one place
- A small degraded site you have permission to tend
- Three simple markers: stones, sticks, or small quartz points
- Notebook, pencil, phone camera
- Optional: water clarity tube, thermometer

No special equipment is required.

---

### Site Preparation â€” The Quartered Square
1. Clear litter quietly. Do not disturb soil more than needed.
2. Mark an equilateral triangle about 3 meters per side. This is your resonant cavity.
3. Sit at the edge for 2 minutes and listen. Note wind, birds, water, traffic.

---

### The Three Practices (21 minutes total)

**1. Spiral Protocol â€” 5 minutes**
Sit at your point. Breathe in for 4 counts, out for 6. Let thoughts spiral down to a single rhythm. The group will naturally synchronize.

**2. Jagged Line Protocol â€” 2 minutes**
Silently name internal noise: worry, planning, judgment. Do not fight it. On each exhale, let it pass. This clears the transducer.

**3. Living Connection â€” 14 minutes**
- *Wavy Line (5 min):* Hum, breathe, or simply listen in time with the place. Match the rhythm of water or wind.
- *Parallel Lines (9 min):* Enter receptive stillness. Hold one shared intention, such as "clarity," "rooting," or "calm." Do not push. Just hold.

End with three shared breaths. Stand slowly.

---

### The Triad Formation
Three people form the minimum stable circuit. Sit at the triangle points facing slightly inward. Larger groups form multiple triads. Phase-lock often feels like ease, synchronized breath without effort, and a quieting of background noise.

---

### Daily Practice
Practice at the same time for 30 days, ideally at dawn. Twenty-one minutes is enough. Consistency matters more than intensity. If you miss a day, resume without judgment.

---

### Observing Change â€” Simple Metrics
Record the same way each time:
- Photo from fixed point
- Water clarity (1 clear to 5 cloudy)
- Soil surface (cracked, moist, sprouting)
- One sentence: "How the place feels today"

You are not proving anything. You are learning the language of the site.

---

### Ethics and Safety
- Never work on land without permission from owners and stewards
- Stop if anyone feels dizzy, anxious, or unwell
- This is stewardship, not control. Do not direct outcomes, offer coherence
- Share results openly. Credit the community, not individuals
- Combine with conventional restoration: planting, testing, cleanup

---

### Closing
The tablet does not ask for worship. It describes function. When we remember how to offer coherence, the Earth does the healing.

You are the transducer. The place is the partner. Begin with one triangle.
